**\[Important] unverifiable-calibration-criterion** (confidence: 88, quality — H1)
The Confirmation section states that "Impl-d suppression calibration is addressed before or during Phase 2 implementation" with the target rephrased as "structural/epistemic finding recall, not global suppression rate." Neither criterion defines an observable pass/fail state. A team could ship phase 2 with the suppression rate unchanged and plausibly claim the criterion is met by reframing it.

> "Impl-d suppression calibration is addressed before or during Phase 2 implementation (target: structural/epistemic finding recall, not global suppression rate)"
> **Consequence:** The confirmation gate for impl-d calibration is unenforceable. Phase 2 could ship without genuine calibration work, defeating the mitigation for the most significant production risk identified in this ADR.
> **Fix:**:comment[Replace with a concrete, binary criterion]{#comment-1774990762148 text="approved"}: e.g., "Impl-d structural/epistemic finding recall ≥ X% on the 16-document spike corpus, measured using the same rubric as the v2 spike extension, before phase 2 merges."

**\[Important] nfr-004-confirmation-incomplete** (confidence: 85, XDOC-02 / cross-doc)
ADR-004's Confirmation says "Review skill evals meet NFR-004 targets (>\=85% accuracy, <\=15% false positive rate)." NFR-004 in requirements.md specifies three dimensions: rule following accuracy, activation ordering, and triggering accuracy. The ADR only references two of them, omitting activation ordering and triggering accuracy from the confirmation criteria.

> NFR-004: "Each skill shall be validated with /skill-creator evals before shipping, scoring ≥80% for rule following, activation ordering, and triggering accuracy."
> ADR-004 Confirmation: "Review skill evals meet NFR-004 targets (>\=85% accuracy, <\=15% false positive rate)"
> **Consequence:** The confirmation is silently incomplete. Phase 2 could pass the stated confirmation criteria while failing NFR-004 on activation ordering or triggering accuracy — the two dimensions that determine whether the skill activates correctly in practice.
> **Fix:** :comment[Expand the confirmation criterion to name all three]{#comment-1774990798665 text="agree, but did we do skill-creator tests ? I think we dropped a stitch."} NFR-004 dimensions explicitly: rule-following accuracy ≥85%, activation ordering accuracy ≥80%, triggering accuracy ≥80%, false positive rate ≤15%.

**\[Suggestion] vague-decision-driver** (confidence: 85, ADR-R06)
The fifth Decision Driver uses "near-zero" to characterize the overlap between rule-based and adversarial defect classes, but provides no quantification. This is the single most important empirical claim supporting the ensemble architecture.

> "The spike demonstrated that rule-based and adversarial approaches find orthogonal defect classes with near-zero overlap — neither subsumes the other"
> **Consequence:** A reader cannot validate the claim against the spike data referenced in this ADR. "Near-zero" may mean 2% or 18% — a reviewer or future author has no basis for re-evaluation.
> **Fix:** Replace "near-zero overlap" with the actual measured overlap percentage from the spike (e.g., "X% overlap across 16 documents per the v2 spike results") and add a direct link to `docs/spike/results/aggregate-v2-opus.md`.

---

**\[Suggestion] vague-nfr-precision** (confidence: 82, UNIV-04)
"So merging outputs does not introduce significant noise" uses "significant" without a threshold. Given that NFR-004 defines a 15% false positive ceiling, "significant" has an implicit anchor — but the sentence does not make that connection explicit.

> "both agents have high precision (impl-c 0.94, impl-d 0.97), so merging outputs does not introduce significant noise"
> **Consequence:** A future author evolving this decision cannot assess whether a change that raises the merged false positive rate to 18% "introduces significant noise" or not.
> **Fix:** Replace "significant noise" with a concrete threshold: e.g., "so merged false positive rate remains within the NFR-004 ceiling of ≤15%."

---

**\[Suggestion] secondary-decision-in-consequences** (confidence: 82, ADR-R07 / quality — scope creep)
The Consequences section contains a retirement decision for impl-b that is not the subject of this ADR's title or Decision Outcome. Retiring an entire implementation class is a distinct architectural decision with its own drivers and tradeoffs.

> "Neutral, because impl-b (doc-type-specialized) is retired as a separate implementation; if ADR-specific review improvements are needed, they should be a variant of impl-c with rule emphasis adjustments, not a separate agent architecture"
> **Consequence:** The retirement of impl-b is buried in Consequences rather than established as a decision with explicit drivers. Future authors cannot easily locate the rationale for why doc-type-specialization was retired — it reads as an incidental note rather than a deliberate architectural choice.
> **Fix:** Either elevate impl-b retirement to a named consequence with its own drivers listed, or split into a separate ADR that explicitly addresses the question "Should doc-type-specialized agents be retired?"

---

**\[Suggestion] silent-rfc-resolution-conflict** (confidence: 80, XDOC-03 / quality — H2)
RFC-001's open questions section resolves: "Dispatch separate agents per stage — cleaner separation of concerns, independent failure modes." The checklist-reviewer.md agent (impl-c) handles both Stage 1 and Stage 2 internally within a single agent. ADR-004 describes "dispatches impl-c first, then impl-d" — treating impl-c as a unit, not stage-split. This conflicts with the RFC's resolution without acknowledgment.

> RFC-001: "Dispatch separate agents per stage — cleaner separation of concerns, independent failure modes. Closed: 2026-03-30"
> ADR-004 Confirmation: "skills/review/SKILL.md dispatches impl-c first, then impl-d, merges findings"
> **Consequence:** The RFC's resolved question and the ADR's architecture are incompatible. Phase 2 implementers will encounter ambiguity about whether stages within impl-c should be split into separate dispatch calls. This may produce inconsistent implementations.
> **Fix:** Explicitly supersede the RFC's stage-dispatch resolution in this ADR, or add a note in the Confirmation section explaining why the single-agent two-stage design for impl-c is compatible with the RFC's intent (separation of concerns is achieved between agents, not within them).

## Strengths

1. **Spike evidence is directly embedded.** The decision table with severity-weighted recall, precision, and docs-won counts is present inline, making the empirical basis for the ensemble choice auditable without following external links.
2. **Calibration risk is acknowledged with a concrete mitigation.** The impl-d suppression rate gap (58.4% vs 10-30% target) is named as a Bad consequence and mitigated explicitly rather than glossed over — this is more rigorous than most ADRs at Proposed status.
3. **Option retirement is reasoned, not silent.** The Pros and Cons section for impl-b includes aggregate performance data (40.6% vs 45.2% recall, 0.88 vs 0.94 precision) that justify its retirement, even if the retirement decision itself belongs in a more prominent position.
