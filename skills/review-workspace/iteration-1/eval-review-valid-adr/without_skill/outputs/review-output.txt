## Review: adr-002-valid-decision.md (Baseline, No Skill)

**Method:** Ad hoc review — no rules framework, no .preflight/ loaded, no rule IDs

---

### Overall assessment

This ADR is well-structured and substantive. It follows MADR format closely and provides
genuine decision-making value. The issues below are genuine quality gaps, not formatting
nitpicks.

---

### Issues found

**High: Missing explicit link between NFR-002 and the Confirmation section**

The "More Information" footer references NFR-002 in `docs/requirements.md` for latency
targets, and the Confirmation section includes a latency target (p95 < 200ms). These are
the same requirement, but they are not explicitly connected — the Confirmation section
does not cite NFR-002. A reviewer cannot verify that the confirmation criterion is
derived from the stated requirement without cross-referencing. The confirmation criterion
should cite NFR-002 directly, e.g., "Analytics query p95 latency < 200ms (per NFR-002)".

**Medium: RFC-001 reference is dangling**

The footer references "Migration plan tracked in RFC-001" but RFC-001 is not summarized
or linked. A reader cannot tell whether RFC-001 exists, whether it was accepted, or what
the migration plan's current status is. The ADR should include the RFC's current status
(Accepted/Superseded/etc.) and, if possible, a relative path.

**Medium: Confirmation criteria mix post-hoc verification with forward-looking targets**

The ADR status is "Accepted" with date 2026-03-15. The Confirmation section includes
"Migration complete with zero data loss (verified via row counts and spot-checks)" —
written in the past tense as if already done — alongside "No regulatory findings related
to transaction integrity at next audit" which is clearly future-looking. It is ambiguous
whether the migration was already complete at the time of acceptance or whether these are
success criteria to be verified later. The section should be consistent: either mark
criteria as future ("shall verify") or explicitly state which have already been
validated.

**Low: "Neutral" consequence is vague**

"Neutral, because team training on PostgreSQL-specific features (JSONB, window functions)
needed" does not say who owns the training, what the scope is, or when it will happen.
As written it is an observation, not an actionable consequence. Either promote it to a
Bad consequence (it has a real cost) or attach an owner/timeline.

**Low: Decision Drivers lack prioritization**

Four decision drivers are listed with no indication of which are hard requirements versus
preferences. "ACID compliance required for financial transaction records (regulatory)" is
a hard constraint — it eliminates MongoDB and MySQL arguably on its own. "Team has strong
SQL expertise" is a preference. The drivers would be more useful to future readers if
they were ordered or tagged (hard constraint vs. preference), since that affects whether
the decision remains sound if team composition changes.

---

### What is working well

- Three options considered with genuine tradeoffs for each — not a pro-forma rubber stamp
- Chosen option reasoning is explicit and directly maps to the decision drivers
- Consequences section covers good/bad/neutral and is specific
- Frontmatter is complete: status, date, deciders, consulted, informed, type, version, owner
- The ADR is self-contained — a new team member could read it and understand the decision

---

### Summary

5 issues: 1 High, 2 Medium, 2 Low. No blocking defects. The High issue (NFR-002 citation
gap in Confirmation) and the RFC-001 status ambiguity are the only items worth fixing
before this ADR is referenced in downstream documents. The document is substantively
sound.
