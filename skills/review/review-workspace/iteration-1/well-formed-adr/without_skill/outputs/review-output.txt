## Overall assessment

This is a well-structured ADR that follows the MADR (Markdown Any Decision Records) format closely. The document is clear, concise, and covers the essential sections. A few areas could be strengthened.

## Findings

### Medium — Missing "Pros and Cons of the Options" section

The ADR lists three considered options but only states the chosen option and its consequences. Standard MADR format includes a comparison of pros/cons for each option, which helps future readers understand why the alternatives were rejected. Without this, the rationale for dismissing Google Docs templates and the custom web application is implicit rather than explicit.

### Medium — No links or references to related ADRs or upstream decisions

The "More Information" section references FR-001 and FR-002 but provides no links or file paths. For a document meant to serve as a decision record, cross-references should be navigable. If these requirements live in a specific file, the path should be included.

### Low — Consequences lack specificity on mitigation

The "Bad" consequence ("team members unfamiliar with Markdown need onboarding") identifies a cost but does not indicate whether this was accepted as-is or whether a mitigation was planned (e.g., a brief onboarding guide, pairing sessions). Strong ADRs note how negative consequences will be addressed.

### Low — No "Validation" or "Review Date" section

There is no indication of when or how this decision should be revisited. For decisions that could be affected by team growth or tooling changes, a review trigger or date helps prevent stale accepted decisions from persisting unchallenged.

### Low — Frontmatter uses a flat list for decision_makers

This is a stylistic observation. The frontmatter lists decision_makers as a YAML array, which is fine structurally. However, if roles matter (e.g., approver vs. consulted), a richer structure would be more informative.

## What works well

- Clear problem statement that motivates the decision
- Decision drivers are specific and relevant
- Three meaningfully different options were considered
- Consequences are categorized (Good/Bad/Neutral) which aids scanning
- YAML frontmatter includes status and date, supporting automated tooling
- The document ID (ADR-001) is present in the title
