## Review: adr-001-use-markdown-templates.md
Document type: adr | Rules: universal-rules.md, adr-rules.md, cross-doc-rules.md

### Findings

**[Critical] missing-frontmatter-fields** (confidence: 97, UNIV-01)
Frontmatter contains `status`, `date`, and `decision_makers` but is missing required `owner` and `version` fields. UNIV-01 requires: "status (from defined enum for that doc type), date (ISO 8601), owner, and version."
**Consequence:** Automated tooling that reads frontmatter for ownership tracking or version gating will fail to identify the document owner or detect stale versions.
**Fix:** Add `owner: <name>` and `version: "1.0"` fields to the YAML frontmatter.

---

**[Critical] fr-id-mismatch** (confidence: 96, structural)
The document states "Related to FR-001 (scaffold skill) and FR-002 (template management)" but `docs/requirements.md` defines FR-001 as "The system shall accept user input through a web form" and FR-002 as "The system shall validate email addresses before submission." The ADR's description of these requirements does not match their actual definitions.
**Consequence:** Any agent or reviewer tracing requirement coverage will incorrectly link this ADR to unrelated requirements, producing false traceability and masking gaps in actual requirement coverage.
**Fix:** Correct the FR references to cite the actual requirement IDs that correspond to scaffold and template management functionality, or remove the references if those requirements do not yet exist.
[Convergence: flagged by both Layer 1 (cross-doc conflict) and Layer 2 H2 (silent conflict)]

---

**[Important] missing-per-option-pros-cons** (confidence: 95, ADR-R04)
The "Considered Options" section lists three options but provides no Pro/Con analysis per option. The "Consequences" section only covers the chosen option. ADR-R04 requires: "Each option SHALL have at least one Pro and one Con."
**Consequence:** Reviewers and future decision-makers cannot evaluate whether alternatives were genuinely assessed or dismissed without analysis, weakening the decision's credibility and making it harder to revisit.
**Fix:** Add a subsection under each considered option with at least one Pro and one Con for each alternative.

---

**[Important] status-enum-case-mismatch** (confidence: 82, ADR-R01)
Frontmatter has `status: accepted` (lowercase). ADR-R01 specifies the enum as: "Proposed, Accepted, Deprecated, Superseded" (title case).
**Consequence:** Automated tooling or rules that match status values case-sensitively will fail to recognize this ADR's status, potentially excluding it from status-filtered queries or reports.
**Fix:** Change `status: accepted` to `status: Accepted` in the frontmatter.

### Strengths
- Clear, neutral context section that describes the problem without advocating for the outcome
- Three distinct alternatives considered spanning different technology approaches (version-controlled files, cloud docs, custom app)
- Consequences section includes both positive and negative outcomes with a balanced "Neutral" entry acknowledging ongoing maintenance cost
