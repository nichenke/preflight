== Findings ==

Critical
--------
- Missing document structure: No introduction, scope, glossary, or document metadata.
  A requirements document needs context for readers to understand what system is
  being specified, who the stakeholders are, and what the boundaries are.

- FR-001 is untestable and meaningless: "The system shall exist" conveys no
  behavioral, functional, or quality requirement. A requirement must describe
  observable behavior or a measurable property. This requirement cannot be
  validated, verified, or used to drive implementation.

- Single requirement: A document with one trivial requirement suggests the
  specification has not been started in any meaningful sense. There are no
  non-functional requirements, no error handling, no constraints.

High
----
- No requirement categorization: Requirements should be organized into sections
  (functional, non-functional, constraints, interfaces) so reviewers and
  implementers can navigate them.

- No EARS pattern usage: The requirement uses bare "shall" without any triggering
  condition (When/While/Where/If). Even ubiquitous requirements benefit from
  stating the system name explicitly: "The <system-name> shall <response>."

- No traceability setup: No links to architecture, design decisions, or test
  strategy. Requirements exist to be traced through implementation and
  verification.

Medium
------
- Bullet-list format instead of structured table or numbered sections: Requirements
  documents benefit from a tabular or structured format with columns for ID,
  description, priority, source, and verification method.

- No version or status metadata: No draft/approved status, no author, no
  revision history.

Low
---
- No glossary or definitions section for domain terms.

== Summary ==

This document is a minimal placeholder, not a reviewable requirements specification.
It contains a single requirement that is tautological and untestable. The document
lacks structure, categorization, traceability, and any meaningful behavioral
specification. It would need to be rewritten from scratch to serve as a
requirements document.
